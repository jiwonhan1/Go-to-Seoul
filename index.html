24-Hour City

Whatever you want, at any time of day or night, Seoul can provide. An early-morning temple visit can lead to a palace tour followed by tea-sipping in Bukchon and gallery-hopping in Samcheong-dong. Soju (a vodka-like drink) and snacks in a street tent bar will fuel you for shopping at the buzzing Dongdaemun or Namdaemun night markets, partying in Hongdae or Itaewon, or singing in a self-service karaoke noraebang. Follow this with steaming, soaking and snoozing in a jjimjilbang (sauna and spa). By the time you look at your watch, it will be dawn again.

What To Do

It's exciting to plan what to do when you are taking a journey to a new country. Seoul is complex and has diverse colors. Ready to dive into Seoul?

Historyhistory

Seoul was a capital city in Chosun Dynasty. There are palaces around the city.

Gyeongbokgung Palace

The main royal palace of the Joseon dynasty.

Address: 161 Sajik-ro, Sejongno, Jongno-gu, Seoul, South Korea

Deoksugung

A walled compound of palaces in Seoul that was inhabited by members of Korea's royal family during the Joseon monarchy.

Address: 199 Sejong-daero, Jeong-dong, Jung-gu, Seoul, South Korea

Gwanghwamun Gate

The main and largest gate of Gyeongbokgung Palace, in Jongno-gu, Seoul, South Korea.

Address: 161 Sajik-ro, Sejongno, Jongno-gu, Seoul, South Korea

Arts & Culturebrush

Want to experience various arts and culture in Seoul? There are many spots to go!

N Seoul Tower

A communication and observation tower located on Namsan Mountain in central Seoul, South Korea.

Address: 161 Sajik-ro, Sejongno, Jongno-gu, Seoul, South Korea

Bukchon Hanok Village

A Korean traditional village in Seoul with a long history located on the top of a hill between Gyeongbok Palace, Changdeok Palace and Jongmyo Royal Shrine.

Address: 37 Gahoe-dong, Jongno-gu, Seoul, South Korea

Myeong-dong

A commercial area, being one of Seoul's main shopping, parade route and tourism districts.

Address: Myeong-dongJung-gu, Seoul, South Korea
